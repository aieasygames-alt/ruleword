Tetris Game Guide

📖 About

Tetris is the legendary puzzle game that has defined the genre for over four decades! Guide falling tetrominos (geometric shapes made of four blocks) as they descend, rotating and positioning them to create complete horizontal lines. When a line is complete, it disappears, scoring points and making room for more pieces. As the game progresses, the pieces fall faster, testing your reflexes, spatial reasoning, and strategic planning. From its Soviet origins in 1984 to its status as a global cultural phenomenon, Tetris remains the gold standard of puzzle gaming.

🎯 Objectives

Survive as long as possible by clearing horizontal lines to prevent the stack from reaching the top. Score points by clearing multiple lines at once—especially the coveted four-line 'Tetris'. Challenge yourself to beat your high score and improve your speed and accuracy.

🎮 How to Play

Use arrow keys to move and rotate falling blocks. Left/Right to move, Up to rotate, Down to soft drop. Complete horizontal lines are cleared. The game speeds up as you progress.

⌨️ Controls

← → Arrow keys move pieces left/right. ↑ Arrow rotates clockwise. ↓ Arrow soft drops (faster fall). Space bar hard drops (instant placement). Hold key stores a piece for later use.

⚙️ Game Mechanics

  • ▸ Seven unique tetrominos (I, O, T, S, Z, J, L) fall from the top
  • ▸ Complete horizontal lines (10 blocks wide) are cleared
  • ▸ Clearing 4 lines at once is called a 'Tetris' and scores maximum points
  • ▸ Game speed increases with level progression
  • ▸ Game ends when pieces stack to the top of the playfield

✨ Features

  • ★ Hold System - Save a piece for later use
  • ★ Ghost Piece - Preview where your piece will land
  • ★ Next Piece Preview - See upcoming pieces
  • ★ Level Progression - Speed increases as you score

💡 Tips

  • ✓ Leave space for the long I-piece on one side for Tetris opportunities
  • ✓ Avoid creating holes that can't be filled
  • ✓ Think ahead about where each piece will fit best
  • ✓ Use hard drop for faster placement when certain

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Tetris?
A Tetris is when you clear 4 lines simultaneously using the long I-piece. It's the highest-scoring move in the game!
How do I improve at Tetris?
Practice regularly, learn to use the hold function strategically, and always think a few pieces ahead. Avoid creating gaps in your stack.
What's the best strategy for beginners?
Keep your stack flat and avoid creating holes. Learn to rotate pieces in both directions for more placement options.
Why does the game get faster?
As you clear more lines and advance levels, the speed increases to provide greater challenge. This is a core element of Tetris gameplay!
