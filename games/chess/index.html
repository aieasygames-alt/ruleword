Chess Game Guide

📖 About

Chess is the world's most celebrated strategy game, with a rich history spanning over 1,500 years! Command an army of 16 pieces—pawns, knights, bishops, rooks, queen, and king—on a 64-square battlefield. Each piece moves according to unique rules, creating infinite possibilities for tactical combinations and strategic depth. Whether you're a beginner learning the basics or a master refining your endgame technique, Chess offers endless intellectual challenge and satisfaction.

🎯 Objectives

Checkmate your opponent's king by placing it under attack with no legal escape. Alternatively, force a stalemate or draw. Along the way, develop your pieces efficiently, control key squares, and execute tactical combinations to gain material advantage.

🎮 How to Play

Click a piece to select it, then click a valid square to move. Green dots show valid moves. Capture the enemy king to win. Each piece has unique movement rules.

⌨️ Controls

Click on any of your pieces to select it. Valid moves will be highlighted with green dots. Click a highlighted square to move. Special moves like castling and en passant are supported.

⚙️ Game Mechanics

  • ▸ Pawns move forward one square (two on first move), capture diagonally
  • ▸ Knights move in an L-shape: two squares in one direction, one perpendicular
  • ▸ Bishops move diagonally any number of squares
  • ▸ Rooks move horizontally or vertically any number of squares
  • ▸ Queen combines rook and bishop movement
  • ▸ King moves one square in any direction

💡 Tips

  • ✓ Control the center of the board early (e4, d4, e5, d5)
  • ✓ Develop your knights and bishops before launching attacks
  • ✓ Castle early to protect your king
  • ✓ Think several moves ahead and consider your opponent's responses

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I checkmate?
Checkmate occurs when the king is under attack and cannot escape by moving, blocking, or capturing the attacking piece. This ends the game.
What is castling?
Castling is a special move where the king moves two squares toward a rook, and the rook moves to the square the king crossed. Requirements: neither piece has moved, no pieces between them, king not in or through check.
How can I improve at chess?
Study opening principles, solve tactical puzzles, analyze your games, and practice regularly. Focus on controlling the center, developing pieces, and king safety.
What's en passant?
En passant is a special pawn capture. If an enemy pawn advances two squares and lands beside your pawn, you can capture it 'in passing' on your next move only, as if it moved just one square.
