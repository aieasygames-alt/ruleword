Huarong Pass Game Guide

📖 About

Huarong Pass (华容道) is one of China's most celebrated sliding block puzzles with a legendary historical theme! Help the warlord Cao Cao escape through the Huarong Pass by sliding blocks strategically. The large 2×2 block represents Cao Cao, while smaller blocks represent generals blocking his path. With a rich history dating back to ancient China, this puzzle challenges your strategic thinking and spatial reasoning!

🎯 Objectives

Slide the blocks to create a path for Cao Cao (the large 2×2 red block) to exit through the bottom center opening. Plan your moves carefully—this classic puzzle requires a minimum of 81 moves to solve!

🎮 How to Play

Click a block to select it, then use Arrow Keys to slide it into empty spaces. Only 曹操 (the red 2x2 block) can exit through the bottom center. Plan your moves carefully!

⌨️ Controls

Click on a block to select it, then use arrow keys to slide it into adjacent empty spaces. Only horizontal and vertical movements are allowed. Blocks cannot overlap.

⚙️ Game Mechanics

  • ▸ 4×5 board with various sized blocks
  • ▸ Cao Cao is the 2×2 block that must escape
  • ▸ Generals are 1×2 blocks (vertical or horizontal)
  • ▸ Soldiers are 1×1 blocks
  • ▸ Two empty spaces allow block movement
  • ▸ Only Cao Cao can exit through the bottom center

✨ Features

  • ★ Classic Layout - Traditional starting position
  • ★ Move Counter - Track your efficiency
  • ★ Historical Theme - Based on Romance of Three Kingdoms
  • ★ Undo System - Take back moves

💡 Tips

  • ✓ Clear a path for 曹操 to reach the exit
  • ✓ Vertical blocks are the most challenging to move
  • ✓ Sometimes you need to move blocks away then back
  • ✓ The classic puzzle requires 81+ moves to solve

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What's the story behind this puzzle?
Based on the Romance of Three Kingdoms, Cao Cao was trapped at Huarong Pass after losing the Battle of Red Cliffs. The puzzle recreates his escape attempt!
What's the minimum number of moves?
The classic starting position requires exactly 81 moves minimum. Finding this optimal solution is extremely challenging!
Why are vertical blocks harder to move?
Vertical 1×2 blocks have limited movement options in a narrow board. They often create bottlenecks that require careful planning to navigate around.
Is there only one solution?
No, there are many ways to solve the puzzle, but only one optimal 81-move solution. Most first attempts take 100+ moves.
