Reaction Test Game Guide

📖 About

The Reaction Test is a classic way to measure your reflexes! Wait for the screen to turn from red to green, then click as fast as you can. Your reaction time is measured in milliseconds. The average human reaction time is about 250ms—can you beat that? Track your best times, identify your average, and see how you compare!

🎯 Objectives

Click as quickly as possible when the screen turns green. Achieve the fastest reaction time possible. Track your progress across multiple attempts and try to improve your average response time.

🎮 How to Play

Click Start, then wait for the screen to turn from red to green. Click immediately when you see green. Don't click too early or you'll have to restart.

⌨️ Controls

Click anywhere on the screen or press any key when the screen turns green. Early clicks (before green) require a restart.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What's a good reaction time?
Average is about 250ms. Good is under 200ms. Excellent is under 150ms. Professional gamers often achieve 100-150ms. Under 100ms is exceptional!
Why do I get different times each attempt?
Human reaction time varies naturally due to focus, fatigue, and anticipation. Taking multiple attempts gives you a more accurate picture of your true reaction speed.
Can I improve my reaction time?
Yes! Regular practice, staying relaxed, good sleep, and avoiding distractions all help. However, there's a biological limit—most improvement comes from eliminating delays, not increasing raw speed.
What happens if I click too early?
The test resets and you must start over. This prevents cheating by randomly clicking. Waiting for the actual signal is part of the test!
